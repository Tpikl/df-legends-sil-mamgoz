> *"They pray to gods of truth while monsters wearing their kin's faces hunt them in the night."*

### Theological Summary
The human pantheon is a study in contradictions. Gods of civilization (Iguk - laws, oaths) stand alongside dark patrons (Luthi - sacrifice, thralldom). A god of truth (Pabat) watches over a people plagued by night creature deception. Most troubling: the goddess of deformity (Agwa) is the most fervently worshipped deity among transformed former humans, suggesting her faith may have invited their corruption.

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Race** | Human |
| **Sex** | Female |
| **Spheres** | Deformity |

**Description:**
The most disturbing deity - a goddess who embodies physical corruption, mutation, and wrongness of form. She has no epithets, just her stark name. Most troubling: she is the most fervently worshipped god among transformed humans, suggesting her cult may have predisposed followers to accept monstrous transformation.

**Theological Implication:** Agwa's followers show the strongest faith after transformation. Did worship of deformity prepare them to accept their new forms? Or does she claim monsters as her special children?

## Theological Themes

### Dominant Motifs

- [x] **Light vs Dark** - Islas (death AND day) embodies this tension directly
- [x] **Order vs Chaos** - Iguk (laws) vs Ulet (chaos) and Luthi (thralldom)
- [x] **Truth vs Deception** - Pabat (truth) in a civilization plagued by night creature impostors
- [ ] **Life vs Death** - Tunem (food) vs Islas (death), but not strongly opposed
- [x] **Body Horror** - Agwa (deformity) as a unique, disturbing sphere

### Theological Tensions

1. **The Pabat Paradox**: A god of truth watches over a people deceived by shapeshifting monsters. Either he is impotent, indifferent, or the monsters' deceptions serve some greater truth.

2. **Luthi's Presence**: Why do a civilized people worship a goddess of sacrifice and slavery alongside gods of law and oaths? Perhaps she represents necessary evils, or debts that must be paid.

3. **Agwa's Children**: The goddess of deformity claims the strongest devotion from transformed humans. Did her worship prepare them to accept monstrous forms, or does she simply embrace what others reject?

4. **The Falcon Outsider**: Ulet is not human - he is a peregrine falcon. Why does a human civilization worship an animal god of war and nightmares? Perhaps he represents the predator humans fear becoming.

### Dark Elements

| Deity | Dark Sphere | Implication |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Agwa | Deformity | Worship of wrongness, mutation, corruption of form |
| Luthi | Sacrifice, Thralldom | Blood offerings, acceptance of slavery |
| Nasnok | Revenge | Sanctified violence, cycles of retribution |
| Ulet | Chaos, Nightmares | Worship of disorder and terror |

Four of ten deities have explicitly dark portfolios. This is not a pantheon of light.
