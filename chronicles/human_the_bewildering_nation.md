### Brief Summary
The Bewildering Nation is the sole human civilization in Sil M책mgoz. Once spanning four settlements including a castle, it has been completely destroyed by monster attacks over five centuries. All sites have been abandoned - even Climaxtails, once thought to be the last holdout, now stands empty. The 372 surviving humans exist as a scattered, homeless population, enduring only through their nomadic Fellowship of Nights and their worship of ten primordial deities. Like the dwarves and elves, humanity in Sil M책mgoz has lost all territorial claims.

## Religion & Culture

### Pantheon Overview

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Deity Count** | 10 |
| **Primary Faith** | Polytheistic |
| **Dominant Themes** | Duality (death/day, truth/deception), dark spheres (deformity, thralldom, sacrifice) |

The humans worship nine primordial gods of their own race plus one falcon deity (Ulet). Notable is the presence of dark spheres - Agwa (deformity), Luthi (sacrifice, thralldom), and Nasnok (revenge) - alongside gods of civilization like Iguk (laws, oaths) and Pabat (truth).

*Full pantheon details: [human_pantheon_the_bewildering_nation.md](human_pantheon_the_bewildering_nation.md)*

**Key Deities:** Tunem (#84), Nasnok (#85), Osman (#86), Islas (#87), Agwa (#88), Pabat (#89), Thab (#90), Luthi (#91), Iguk (#92), Ulet (#93)

### Cultural Notes
- Mead halls served as community centers: "The Tiredness of Taxes" and "The Sculpted Sacrifice"
- The tomb complex at Wanderedbanner suggests ancestor veneration
- No known written works from human authors survive in records
- Religious practices may include sacrifice (worship of Luthi)

**Human Sites Destroyed by the Roc:**

### The Titan's Wrath (Year 91)

**Umbrallilac "The Crystalline"** - A titan 330 years old - destroyed the human castle of Charcoalsharks in Year 91. This ended The Council of Stoking and left humanity without a central seat of power.
